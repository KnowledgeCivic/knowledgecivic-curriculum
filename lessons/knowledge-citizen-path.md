# Learning to Be a Knowledge Citizen

Audience: older secondary learners, adults, community groups, and independent learners.

## Outcomes

By the end, learners can frame a public question, distinguish evidence from opinion, locate primary sources, explain uncertainty, represent disagreement fairly, and publish a documented knowledge note.

## Session 1: What is a public question?

Map a local issue. Rewrite broad complaints as questions that could be investigated. Output: one question and a list of affected groups.

## Session 2: Claims, evidence, and uncertainty

Classify five sample statements as observation, prediction, causal explanation, value judgment, or policy proposal. Add one possible test and one limitation for each.

## Session 3: Source tracing

Trace a viral claim to its earliest accessible source. Compare the original with two secondary descriptions. Output: a source map and a reliability note.

## Session 4: Numbers in public life

Practice denominators, rates, time windows, comparison groups, and uncertainty. Rewrite a misleading chart caption without attacking its author.

## Session 5: Disagreement and deliberation

Steelman two reasonable positions. Identify shared facts, different values, and unresolved evidence. Output: a disagreement map.

## Session 6: Publish for the public

Create a 500-800 word knowledge note with sources, date, limitations, license, and a correction route. Peer review it using the checklist in CONTRIBUTING.md.

## Assessment

Use a portfolio rather than a single test: question quality, traceability, uncertainty, fairness, accessibility, and reflection on what changed after review.
